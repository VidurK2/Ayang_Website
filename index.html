About Us

Know More About Us

Welcome to Ayang, where love is more than just a word—it’s a way of life. Embark on a journey with us through the enchanting riverine island of Majuli, where our transformative quest began in the quaint village of Kulamua. Our reach extends far and wide, embracing livelihoods, land rights, and disaster preparedness, empowering lives with every step. Join us, and together, let’s weave a tapestry of change and compassion.

Mission
Bridging developmental gaps in Assam by co-creating solutions with the adversity-affected communities towards improving primary school education through teacher capacity building and enhanced community involvement, and skilling and empowering women artisans and farmers towards economic independence and agency
Vision
A world where everyone has a choice to live a life of love, dignity, and purpose irrespective of conditions at birth

Education Projects

Our initial mobile library project impressed teachers, who then asked for support in other subjects. We expanded our efforts through the Manthan fellowship, a two-year program aimed at developing skills and fostering social change among rural youth, particularly in Majuli. Traditional fellowships often favor candidates from privileged backgrounds, so Manthan aims to bridge this gap.

Livlihood Programs

Our skill-building interventions amplify the local traditions in Majuli through training, the introduction of the latest technologies, and the creation of market linkages. Traditional livelihood training includes 1) weaving, 2) bamboo crafts, and 3) food processing. Additionally, we also do needs assessments and introduce training on livelihood interventions that are less prevalent in local markets in Majuli such as bakery training.

Community Welfare and resilience

More people in the families and villages where our patients come from understand mental illnesses objectively and this has helped combat dangerous superstitions surrounding it. Seeing patient successes, we are organically having families seek our support wherein earlier it was a hushed topic. The project is slowly aiding in destigmatizing mental illnesses in Majuli.
